Red flags
–
Mother Nature does not care about turnarounds
Even with the best AI software and reinsurance shields, severe weather events are highly volatile wildcards that can heavily damage a single quarter's earnings. One catastrophic hurricane season can erase an entire year of disciplined underwriting. This risk never goes away. It only gets managed, never eliminated.
–
Passing the baton to a new CEO mid-momentum
Turnaround mastermind Peter Zaffino steps away from the day-to-day CEO role in June 2026. Incoming CEO Eric Andersen is a seasoned insurance professional, but replacing the architect of a structural transformation at the exact moment profits are accelerating is always a governance risk that deserves close monitoring.
–
The pricing party could slow down by late 2027
Commercial insurance prices have been rising for several consecutive years as the industry repaired from COVID-era losses. If the market cools off or competitors launch a price war, AIG's margin expansion could flatten out. The current combined ratio improvement is partly cyclical, not purely structural. Knowing the difference matters.
